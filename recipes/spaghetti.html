Spaighetti

Description(from the web)

Spaghetti is a long, thin, solid, cylindrical pasta made from durum wheat semolina and water. Its name derives from the Italian word spaghetto, which translates to "little string" or "little cord," perfectly describing its appearance.

Core Ingredients(from the web)

Note: *recipes may vary

  • Pasta: 1 lb spaghetti noodles
  • Meat: 1 lb ground beef (80/20 or 93/7 lean)
  • Tomatoes: 1 (28-oz) can crushed tomatoes or diced tomatoes, 1 (16-oz) can tomato sauce, and 1 (6-oz) can tomato paste
  • Aromatics: 1 yellow onion (chopped), 3-4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • Seasoning: 1 to 2 tsp Italian seasoning, 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp black pepper
  • Flavor Balancer: 1 to 2 tsp sugar (to reduce acidity).

Steps(from the web)

  1. Boil Water: Use a large pot with plenty of water to give the spaghetti room to cook
  2. Salt Heavily: Add a generous amount of salt to the boiling water for flavor
  3. Add Pasta: Add the spaghetti without breaking it in half
  4. Stir: Stir frequently in the first few minutes to prevent sticking
  5. Check for Doneess: Test the pasta starting around 8 minutes to ensure it is al dente firm to the bite
  6. Reserve Water & Drain: Before draining, save about a cup of the starchy pasta water
  7. Finish in Sauce: Combine the drained pasta directly with your sauce, adding a splash of the reserved water to help the sauce adhere
